AI Liquid Cooling System Architecture

This page focuses on rack-level coolant distribution. Modern liquid-cooled AI infrastructure is built as a layered system, from facility-level cooling through rack distribution and server-level thermal transfer.

Step 1

CDU

Controls coolant flow and temperature, circulating fluid between facility water and the rack cooling loop.

Step 2

In-Rack Manifold

Distributes cold coolant evenly across servers and collects return flow.

Step 3

QDC + Routing

Quick disconnects and hoses connect servers to the manifold, enabling modular deployment and serviceability.

Step 4

Cold Plates

Remove heat directly from processors and transfer it into the liquid cooling loop.

The backbone of rack-level cooling

While cold plates remove heat and CDUs control the system, the manifold is what makes rack-level distribution possible. It acts as the central spine of the rack, routing coolant across multiple servers, supporting consistent thermal performance, and creating a common connection point between rack infrastructure and the broader cooling loop.

Manifold role within the rack

Within the rack, the manifold acts as the central distribution spine. It receives coolant from the CDU, routes it evenly across multiple server nodes, and collects return flow for recirculation. This centralized distribution layer enables consistent thermal performance and supports modular, serviceable rack design.

Built for high-density rack environments

Rack manifolds are engineered to operate inside constrained rack spaces while maintaining reliable coolant delivery to every server. In modern AI and HPC deployments, they enable dense configurations without sacrificing accessibility, serviceability, or thermal consistency.

Designed as a common connection point

In-rack manifolds provide the shared interface between CDU-side coolant distribution and server-level liquid cooling hardware. This centralization simplifies rack plumbing, improves deployment alignment, and supports cleaner integration with cold plates, hoses, valves, and quick disconnect systems.
