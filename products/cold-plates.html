Cold plate position within the liquid cooling loop

In single-phase direct-to-chip liquid cooling systems, coolant is circulated from the CDU through rack manifolds and server-side liquid routing before reaching the cold plate mounted on the processor. At the cold plate, heat is transferred from the chip into the coolant, which then returns through the loop for heat rejection.

Step 1

CDU

The cooling distribution unit circulates coolant and interfaces the rack loop with facility-side cooling infrastructure.

Step 2

Rack Manifold

The manifold distributes cold coolant to multiple compute nodes and collects warm return fluid across the rack.

Step 3

Server Liquid Routing

Hoses, fittings, and internal liquid routing assemblies carry coolant from the rack manifold into each server or processor assembly.

Step 4

Cold Plate Heat Transfer

The cold plate removes heat directly from the processor package, transferring thermal energy into the coolant before it returns through the loop for rejection.
