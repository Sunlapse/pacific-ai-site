Deployment Components

The hardware that turns liquid cooling design into field deployment.

Deployment components bridge the gap between cooling architecture and real installation. They help connect racks, route coolant, support service access, and reduce integration friction during project rollout.

Built for practical rack deployment.

Liquid cooling projects depend on more than core hardware. Hose assemblies, fittings, valves, QDCs, and routing components determine how cleanly the system installs and scales.

Where deployment components fit.

Deployment components support the physical connections between the CDU, rack manifold, server routing, and cold plate assemblies. They make the system installable, serviceable, and repeatable across racks.

Step 1

CDU

The cooling distribution unit provides coolant circulation and connects rack-side liquid cooling to facility-side systems.

Step 2

Rack Manifold

The manifold distributes coolant across rack positions and collects return flow from multiple compute nodes.

Step 3

Deployment Hardware

Hoses, QDCs, valves, fittings, brackets, and routing components connect the physical liquid cooling system in the field.

Step 4

Cold Plates

Cold plates remove heat directly from GPU and CPU packages before coolant returns through the rack loop.

What It Includes

Hoses, fittings, valves, and QDCs.

Deployment components include the practical hardware used to route coolant, connect racks, isolate service points, and support phased installation.

Why It Matters

Clean installs reduce field friction.

The right deployment hardware can reduce installation complexity, improve serviceability, and help keep rack-level liquid cooling programs consistent.

Small parts become critical when racks must be installed, serviced, expanded, or replaced under tight timelines.

Installation Support

Rack-to-CDU connection readiness.

Deployment components help connect CDU outputs, rack manifolds, in-rack routing, and server-side liquid cooling assemblies.

This is especially important for retrofit programs, hybrid air-to-liquid deployments, and phased AI rack rollouts.

Deployment hardware can be configured around rack layout, coolant routing, CDU position, manifold geometry, service access, and installation sequence.
