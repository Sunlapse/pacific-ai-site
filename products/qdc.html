Where QDCs are used across the liquid cooling system

Quick disconnect couplings are not limited to a single point in the cooling loop. In direct-to-chip liquid cooling architectures, QDCs are deployed across multiple interface layers to enable secure fluid connections, modular system design, and fast serviceability without draining the loop.

Interface 1

CDU to Rack Manifold

QDCs are often used between the cooling distribution unit and rack manifolds, allowing racks to be connected or isolated without disrupting facility-side cooling infrastructure.

Interface 2

Rack Manifold Distribution

Within the rack, manifolds distribute cold coolant and collect warm return fluid. QDCs enable modular connections to each compute node.

Interface 3

Server & Node Connections

QDCs are used to connect individual server trays or GPU nodes, allowing systems to be installed, removed, or serviced without breaking the entire cooling loop.

Interface 4

Cold Plate Loop Integration

In some designs, QDCs are positioned near cold plate interfaces or internal hose assemblies to support platform-specific routing and maintenance flexibility.

Connector types by application

Quick disconnect coupling is the system-level category. Within that category, different connector series are typically selected based on flow requirement, physical space, serviceability, and the specific connection point within the cooling architecture.

Connection point Typical QDC type What usually matters
CDU to rack manifold Higher-flow QDC, often UQD series Larger flow capacity, low restriction, reliable rack isolation
Rack manifold to server Mid-size QDC, often UQD or similar formats Balanced flow, compact fit, repeatable service connections
Server or node-level liquid routing Compact QDC, often MQD or similar formats Dense spacing, ease of maintenance, modular replacement
Cold plate loop integration Application-specific compact QDC or fitting configuration Platform fit, routing flexibility, service access
