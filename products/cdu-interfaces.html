What the CDU does in the cooling system

The coolant distribution unit is the operational control point of the secondary cooling loop. It isolates rack-side coolant from facility water, transfers heat through a heat exchanger, circulates coolant with pumps, and manages flow and temperature to support stable direct-to-chip liquid cooling performance.

Where CDU interfaces fit in the liquid cooling loop

CDU interfaces sit at the transition between the rack cooling loop and the CDU. These connection points support clean supply and return routing, service access, modular rack deployment, and integration with manifolds, hoses, valves, and quick disconnect assemblies.

Step 1

Facility Water

Building-side chilled water or heat rejection infrastructure supplies the primary loop to the CDU.

Step 2

CDU

The CDU decouples the primary and secondary loops, controls coolant conditions, and circulates clean coolant through the rack-side loop.

Step 3

CDU Interface Layer

Connection hardware and interface assemblies route supply and return lines between the CDU and rack manifold infrastructure.

Step 4

Rack Cooling Loop

Coolant flows through manifolds, hoses, QDCs, and cold plates, then returns warmed coolant back through the CDU for heat rejection.

Designed for decoupled liquid cooling systems

CDU-connected liquid cooling systems rely on a decoupled architecture in which the CDU separates facility water from the rack-side loop. Interface hardware at this boundary must support reliable routing, compatibility with secondary loop components, and clean integration into the wider cooling system.
