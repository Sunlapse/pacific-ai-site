CDU Interfaces

The connection point between the CDU and the rack.

CDU interface components help liquid-cooled racks connect cleanly into coolant distribution systems with the correct routing, access, fittings, and deployment alignment.

Built for decoupled liquid cooling systems.

The CDU separates facility water from the rack-side cooling loop. Interface hardware at this boundary needs to fit the deployment architecture, not just the component drawing.

Where CDU interfaces fit in the loop.

The interface layer sits after the CDU and before the rack cooling loop. This is where connection hardware, routing, hoses, valves, and manifold integration need to line up.

Step 1

Facility Water

Building-side chilled water or heat rejection infrastructure supplies the primary loop to the CDU.

Step 2

CDU

The CDU decouples the primary and secondary loops, controls coolant conditions, and circulates clean coolant.

Step 3

Interface Layer

CDU interface assemblies route supply and return lines between the CDU and rack manifold infrastructure.

Step 4

Rack Cooling Loop

Coolant flows through manifolds, hoses, QDCs, and cold plates, then returns warmed coolant back through the CDU.

Why It Matters

Clean rack-to-CDU connection.

CDU interfaces help avoid mismatch between the CDU, manifold layout, hose routing, valve placement, and field installation requirements.

Important for high-density GPU racks where service access, flow path clarity, and deployment consistency matter.

Typical Applications

Rack cooling integration.

Used in CDU-connected liquid cooling systems, in-rack CDU layouts, floor-mounted CDU architectures, and direct-to-chip AI rack deployments.

Often aligned with manifolds, hoses, valves, quick disconnects, and rack-level routing hardware.
